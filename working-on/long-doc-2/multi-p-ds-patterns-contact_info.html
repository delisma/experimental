Contact us: Government of Canada design system

Contact us information can be presented with a number of different patterns. The pattern can be used for street or mailing addresses, email addresses, telephone numbers, online alternatives to contact us and can alternatively be used to present links to further contact pages.

On this page

  • When to use
  • How to implement
  • Research
  • Latest updates
  • Discussion

When to use

Use the appropriate contact us pattern to present contact information:

Contact links
for links to contact pages for various tasks
Address
for an address and limited additional contact details
Phone
to show details about how to contact the government by phone for an institution or service
Multi-channel
for a phone pattern plus additional self-serve option(s) and/or contact channels
Multi-channel multi-audience
for more than one multi-channel patterns for different audiences or similar contexts

How to implement

Contact blocks should never have borders applied to them.

Links to contact pages

When to use: When a number of different tasks require separate contact us details that would not fit under the multi-channel and multi-audience patterns.

Contact us / Find an office

  • [Institution contact two]

Address

When to use: When there is only an address, or an address and secondary alternatives that have simple, one line descriptions.

Implementation guidelines: when street or mailing address information is available, place it first, followed by other contact channels.

Research

Layout choices
  • Redirecting users to primary self-service option first - online
  • Forcing choices (use of expand collapse for secondary options, smart question for audiences and url parameters) - make sure they get the right number
  • Using an interactive pattern to bring attention to what needs to be prepared before calling/
Use page banding, not borders
  • Borders should not be applied to contact blocks because this will make them more easily missed because of banner blindness
  • This does not apply to page banding, which has been tested successfully for some tasks on Canada.ca
